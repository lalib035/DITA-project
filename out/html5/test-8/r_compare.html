Generic Container Comparison & Examples

HTML has two types of generic containers: in-line (<span>) and block-level (<div>). Examples of their different uses may help you choose the proper generic container.

Element Purpose Example
<span> In-line generic container. <span> should be used to contain small sections of content that do not stand alone. A single letter in a word, a single word in a sentence, or a single sentence in a paragraph.
<div> Block-level generic container. <div> should be used to contain larger blocks or complete sections of content. An entire paragraph, a section of a document.
